Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

For example, sun damage and fading could indicate that you've had your helmet for a long time & therefore is time to switch it up. Things like scuffs and cracks are also a fair indication of extensive wear & damage, meaning you will need to replace your helmet.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If it hasn't been in storage too long and you used a fuel stabilizer before storing your bike you should be ok.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a Helmet. ... Check Your Equipment Before You Ride. ... Wear Reflective Materials. ... Keep Your Hands on the Bike. ... Know Your Signals and Use Them. ... Limit Your Distractions. ... Ride As If You're In a Car. ... Ride With the Flow of Traffic.
